VHS tapes

A Brief History of VHS Tapes | DVD Your MemoriesA VHS (Video Home System) tape is an analog, magnetic tape recording format on a -inch tape housed in a plastic cassette. Introduced by JVC in 1976, it became the dominant home video standard for movies and recording TV shows during the 1980s and 90s. Tapes require a VCR (Video Cassette Recorder) to play, allowing users to watch, record, pause, and rewind, typically holding 2-4 hours of footage.

The GrabPack in Poppy Playtime is a backpack-based utility tool created by Playtime Co. used to interact with the environment from a distance. It features two retractable, colorful hands that can grip, pull, and electrify objects. It is essential for solving puzzles, activating switches, and traversing gaps using its wire-driven hands.
